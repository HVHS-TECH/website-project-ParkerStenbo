Mildly hazardous



What is the Sea Vixen

The Sea vixen is a twin tailed, twin engined, twin seat fighter jet operated by the royal navy from the 1950s to the 1970s.
It was developed by de Havviland as and all weather, jet powered, radar equipped fighter for the royal navy. Because of this, it was designed with two engines for travel over water, the second crew member was to handle navigation and radar equipment, and swept wings allowed high speed travel.
At this time, Britain, like most nations, saw dogfighting as an obsolete practice, believing it would be replaced by bvr combat. This is possibly the reason the sea vixen wasn't equipped with a gun. Insteaed it could carry four early Infra Red guided missiles.
It was eventually replaced by the liscence built Phantom F.G 1 (baseically an f4 Phantom II but with different engines)



My personal opinion

My opinion on this aircrafts design is rather similar to that of the strikemaster.
The aircraft is quite elegant, everything fits together nicely making it look as if it's made of only two parts, the wing, and the tail.
the offset cockpit adds a lot of character and gives the aircraft a unique, intresting, and identifyable profile when paired with the twin-boom tail desing.
Unfortunately, there have been no flying sea vixens since 2017.
